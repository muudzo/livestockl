## 4. Talking points per beat (the spoken spine)

**0 — Hook (0:45).** *"On my second week, someone in this room told me: don't build a thesis, build software you can sell. I took that literally. This is where it went — including the part where I found out my first business plan was wrong."*

**1 — The brief & the field (2:00).** *"So before I wrote a line of code, I sat through a full sale day at a real cattle auction floor."* Land the four findings (US$1,000 deposit gate; 12% house fees pushing the long tail to WhatsApp; dealers-only attendance; a paper, in-person police-clearance bottleneck). *"Four problems. The auction house already owns the trust — they just have no digital surface for it. That's the gap."*

**2 — What I built (5:00).** *"A digital floor that runs under the auction house's own brand, on Paynow rails, reachable on every surface a Zimbabwean buyer actually uses — web, WhatsApp, USSD, BillPay."* → **live demo** → *"And on May 8 you gave me six asks. Five are live: configurable auction mechanics, sellers register a Paynow merchant ID instead of bank details, Bisafe escrow, the WhatsApp bot, USSD bidding. The sixth — Paab cash — is waiting on you, and it's my first ask later."*

**3 — What I learned (3:00).** Two lessons, both honest.
- *"Technical: Paynow Core sits behind a Cloudflare bot wall that kills any request from a datacenter IP — Supabase, Render, Railway, all blocked. I worked around it with a 70-line Cloudflare Worker. But BillPay, on a sub-host, integrated direct in 90 minutes. The fix is one DNS change — do for Core what you already did for BillPay, and you unblock every Zimbabwean dev trying to build agentic payments. That's written up for your engineers."*
- *"Market: my first financial model was a first-world hockey stick — ten houses, seventy-percent digital adoption, half a million in revenue. It was wrong. You don't burn capital toward a break-even month in a market with no venture capital and scarce USD. So I rebuilt it as what this actually is."*

**4 — The business, honestly (4:00).** *"A bootstrap. No raise, no founder salary — my income is the operating surplus. Cash-positive from the first live house. The per-house unit works even at fifteen-percent adoption. The constraint isn't adoption — doubling it adds two hundred dollars a month — it's how many houses one operator can run well. And the number that matters to you:"* → **slide 7** *"the GMV we route onto Paynow rails: forty-three thousand, then two-twenty, then five-forty-five — and that's the line that grows fastest."*

**5 — The ask (2:00).** *"I'm not here for money. I'm here for doors and rails."* → the three asks (§5).

**6 — Close (0:30).** *"Per-house economics work, growth pays for itself, and the ceiling is people, not cash. Build software you can sell — and a business this market can actually carry. Thank you."*

## 5. The three asks (land these crisply — this is the point of the whole demo)

1. **Unblock the rails.** Paab sandbox + docs (the only red on the board) and the BillPay **PAY** round-trip (vendor-portal registration with rotated creds — AUTH is already live). *Both widen the buyer base and lift adoption.*
2. **Formalize the partnership.** Name ZimLivestock as Paynow's **livestock vertical solution**; Paynow BD refers livestock-industry prospects. *We grow your GMV; you grow our pipeline.*
3. **Open one door.** A warm intro to **one Harare-area auction house.** *The pilot is paid by the customer, not by Paynow — I just need the room.*

End on: *"Three things, none of them a cheque."*

## 6. Q&A prep

**Business (new — rehearse these):**
- **"Why should we believe 15% adoption, not higher?"** → *Field research: buyers pay US$1,000 cash deposits and show up in person. Trust moves slowly. 15% is the conservative ceiling; if it's higher, that's upside — but the model doesn't depend on it, because revenue is retainer-led.*
- **"This looks small — $32k in year 3?"** → *On purpose. It's a bootstrap, not a fundraise. The per-house unit works; I add houses from surplus. The number that scales for you is the GMV on your rails — $545k by year 3 and climbing. The small number is the founder's honest income; the big number is yours.*
- **"What do you actually need from us?"** → the three asks. *None is capital.*
- **"Why not just let Paynow build this?"** → *Your strategic position is infrastructure, not running auction floors. That's exactly why this is a partnership, not competition — I route volume onto your rails and do the on-the-ground operating you don't want to.*
- **"What's the retainer buy them?"** → *We run the platform on their behalf — monitoring, support, reconciliation, monthly reporting. They run the floor; we run every screen.*

**Product / DX (reuse from `final-demo-script.md` §Q&A):** scaling, "what if Paynow goes down" (poll-sync + Stripe + reconcile), testing without burning EcoCash (test numbers `0771111111` etc.), "why React + Supabase not Go," the relay URLs (offer to transfer to Paynow's account).

## 8. The two things that win or lose this room
