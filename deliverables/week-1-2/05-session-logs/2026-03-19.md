# Session Log — March 19, 2026 (Thursday)
## Week 1, Day 6 — Field Research: Auction House Visit

### What I Did
- **Visited a physical livestock auction house** in person
- Documented 8 key findings from the visit:
  1. US$1,000 deposit required to bid (massive barrier to entry)
  2. 12% fee structure (6% buyer + 6% seller)
  3. ~45 active bidders per auction (liquidity benchmark)
  4. Police clearance required before animal leaves
  5. Cash-dominant — no digital payment infrastructure
  6. Transport is a separate, unstructured market
  7. Stock cards (vet certificates) are the trust mechanism
  8. Auctions only run on Wednesdays at one location
- Identified 2 transport truckers as partnership leads
- Added staged clearance strategy for digital equivalent
- Created 6-week roadmap with daily tasks, metrics, and branch cleanup plan

### Key Insights
- The deposit barrier is the #1 thing we can disrupt (US$50 via tokenization vs US$1,000 cash)
- "Sell cattle, pay school fees" is a real behavior — multiple farmers mentioned it
- Professional dealers dominate the auction floor; small farmers are priced out
