# ZimLivestock — Engagement Execution Playbook

**The 3-week pitch sprint, and the per-stakeholder methods underneath it.**

**Version:** v1.0 · **Author:** Tatenda Nyemudzo · **Date:** June 2026
**Companion:** [stakeholder-engagement-plan.md](./stakeholder-engagement-plan.md) (the strategy — *who, why, what order*). This doc is the execution layer: *how, starting now, with a countdown to first pitches.*

> The strategy says land the house, then activate the catchment, then layer transport. This says: here is exactly what you do Monday morning — who you call, what you say, what you build, and how you get good enough to be believed in three weeks.

---

## Five operating principles (your words, turned into rules)

These come straight from how you framed this. They govern every section below.

1. **Repetition is the system.** Not a motivational line — the actual method. Pitch reps, outreach volume, content cadence. The Elon Musk arc (halting in 2008, fluent now) is not talent; it is thousands of reps in public. You compress that: ~50 recorded pitch reps before the first real meeting. *More swings = more contact.*
2. **Do things that don't scale** (Paul Graham). The first houses and the first sellers are won **by hand, in person, one at a time.** Do not build funnels yet. Airbnb's founders went door-to-door in New York, photographed listings themselves, and funded the company selling cereal — *then* it scaled. Your equivalent: go back to the auction floor, shake hands, onboard the first seller yourself.
3. **Be lookup-able.** The night before your pitch, they will Google you. If they find nothing, you're a student with an app. If they find a sharp LinkedIn, a clean one-page site, and a trail of build-in-public posts, you're an operator building something real. **Manufacture legitimacy before you walk in.**
4. **Be memorable (consumer side).** Humour → memory → share. A person who laughs *remembers*, and a person who remembers *tags someone*. Engineer for shares and comments — not likes.
5. **Go where you get headway.** Start with the gettable yes, not the hardest anchor. A reference in hand turns the anchor pitch from a cold sell into a "everyone's moving" sell.

## PART 1 — The 3-week sprint

Three weeks ≠ signed deals. Three weeks = **booked meetings, a deck you can deliver in your sleep, ~50 reps, a lookup-able you, and a captured pipeline.** Honest goal-setting; signatures come on the pilot clock after.

| | Theme | Build | Outreach | Reps & brand |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Week 1** *(Foundations)* | Get armed | Finalise 6-slide deck (Part 4). Pick + lock the story. Stand up LinkedIn + one-page site (Part 3). | Build the **First-15 target list** (Part 2). Map warm-intro paths. Join the FB/WhatsApp groups (lurk, don't pitch yet). | Start reps: **3 recorded pitches/day.** First build-in-public post. |
| **Week 2** *(Contact)* | Get in rooms | Tighten deck from rep feedback. Booth kit if an event falls in-window (banner + capture). | **Warm outreach:** Paynow BD intros, the field-visit house, group admins. Book ≥5 meetings. Soft-DM 10 sellers from groups. | Reps continue (now ~30 logged). 2–3 build-in-public posts. Launch Mimoo teaser (Part 2.3). |
| **Week 3** *(Pitch)* | Deliver & capture | Print one-pager leave-behind. | **Pitch.** Run the meetings. Work the booth/event. Capture every contact (name, house, phone, pain). Same-day follow-up. | ~50 reps total. Post the pitch journey. Keep the content engine running. |

**The single most important Week-1 action:** lock the story and start recording reps *that day*. Everything else can slip a day; the reps can't — they need the full three weeks to compound.

## PART 2 — Per-stakeholder execution methodology

Different stakeholders, genuinely different methods. Do not run one playbook across all three.

### 2.1 Auction houses — the founder sell (high-touch, by hand)

**Targeting: who says yes first.** Score every prospect on the *gettable-yes* profile:

| Signal | Gettable (target first) | Hard (save for later) |
|---|---|---|
| Size | Mid / small (Tier B/C) — feels the squeeze, less to lose | Dominant anchor — thinks it doesn't need you |
| Decision-maker | Younger owner, or the **son/successor** running ops | Old-guard principal, slow to change |
| Pain | Already losing trades to WhatsApp; complains about no-shows / slow cash | Comfortable, full rooms |
| Access | You have a warm intro or rapport (**the house from your 19 March field visit**) | Cold, no connection |
| Geography | Your home region — relationship leverage | Far, no roots |

> **Reconciliation with the strategy:** v3.0 is anchors-first *for the year*. For the *first pitch*, invert it — win a gettable house as your **reference/pilot**, then walk into the anchor with proof. This directly de-risks the "3 anchors in Year 1" assumption the model itself flagged as most aggressive.

**Access map — easiest contact paths, ranked.** You asked for the easiest way to reach people. In order:

| Rank | Path | Why it's easy | First action |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Paynow BD warm intros** | You're the intern — *use the badge.* Ask who they know in livestock settlement. | Email your Paynow contacts this week. |
| 2 | **The field-visit house** | You already have rapport and saw their floor. | Call, say you built the thing you discussed, ask for 20 min. |
| 3 | **Auctioneers are public** | Houses advertise sale days on Facebook with phone numbers + catalogues. | DM/call three from their own FB pages. |
| 4 | **ZLPA / livestock producers' body** | Member directory = a target list handed to you. | Get the member list; sponsor/speak. |
| 5 | **AGRITEX / vet officers** | They know every house and farmer in the district. | Ask one officer for three introductions. |
| 6 | **FB/WhatsApp livestock groups** | Admins are super-connectors. | DM the admins (Part 2.2). |
| 7 | **Your own network** | Zimbabwe is relationship-dense — family, church, uni. | Ask 5 people "who do you know with cattle?" |

**The conversation arc** (not a monologue — diagnose, then pitch): Saturday floor visit → quantify their pain out loud (no-shows, cash handling, thin rooms) → the 6-slide deck → the **90-day pilot at US$1,000 creditable**: *"Let's run ONE sale day with remote bidders added, and I'll show you the buyers you're missing. No risk — the fee credits to your contract if you continue."*

**Money on the table (the slide that lands).** Parameterise to the house in front of you:
- A house clearing ~US$100k/sale-day × ~4/mo ≈ **US$4.8M/yr** across the floor.
- A thin room clears low. **More bidders lift hammer prices** — every auctioneer knows this in their bones.
- A modest **3% price lift** on US$4.8M = **+US$144k GMV/yr** → at their 12% commission, **~US$17k more commission a year** — for a subscription of US$900–1,500/mo.
- Frame: *"For every buyer in your pen on Saturday, there's another who wanted in but couldn't make the US$1,000 deposit or the drive from town. That demand doesn't vanish — it leaks to WhatsApp, unsettled, at worse prices. I bring it back into your room."*

The emotive story lives in Part 4 (deck script).

### 2.2 Sellers — excitement + unscalable trust

Sellers don't respond to a sell; they respond to **excitement and proof their neighbour got paid.**

**Do-things-that-don't-scale recruitment.** The first sellers are onboarded by *you*, by hand:
- Go to a sale day. Find a farmer about to sell. Offer to list their beast digitally **for them**, on the spot, and show them the EcoCash hit when it clears. (Airbnb photographed the first listings themselves — you do the first listings.)
- That one farmer telling the dip-tank queue "*the money was in my phone before I left the pen*" is worth more than any ad. Manufacture that first story deliberately.

**The Facebook/WhatsApp group play (intel + leverage).** You're right that these groups are gold. Two jobs:

1. **Intelligence (Week 1, lurk):** Which houses are named? What do sellers complain about (prices, scams, no-shows, slow payment)? What do buyers ask for? Who are the admins/super-connectors? Screenshot recurring pain — it's free pitch material and deck copy in the sellers' own words.
2. **Leverage (the data point you raised):** these groups *are* an unsettled, scam-prone marketplace already doing real volume with **no payment guarantee.** That is precisely the gap you close. Don't spam them — offer the admins something: *"I built a tool that guarantees your members get paid. Want to pilot it with your group?"* The admin becomes your channel.

> Tasteful rule: infiltrate to *learn and serve*, not to scrape and spam. The trust you're selling is the asset — don't burn it on day one.

**The ag-event booth (your in-person seller funnel).** Get a stand at a provincial show or livestock field day in-window if one exists; if not, target the next one and prep now.
- **Banner: 3 bullets, no more.** Big, readable from 5 metres. Draft:
  - **"Sell your cattle to more buyers."**
  - **"Get paid before you leave — on EcoCash."**
  - **"No bank account needed."**
- **Capture everything.** A clipboard *and* a phone-number capture (a USSD short-string or "WhatsApp this number"). Tiny incentive — a raffle for branded merch, or airtime — to make people leave a number. Goal: walk away with **100+ contacts**, each tagged seller/buyer + region.
- Live demo: list a real animal on the spot; show the payout flow.

**Excitement engine (social, for sellers).** Sellers want aspiration + proof: record-price reveals ("*this bull sold for US$X*"), payout-notification moments, farmer testimonials in vernacular. Repetition: post the *same* proof beats weekly — familiarity builds trust in a low-trust market.

### 2.3 Consumers — the Mimoo meme engine (aggressive, virality-seeking)

This is the "educate the masses" layer, and per your steer: **most aggressive social approach, more is better, be memorable, work toward virality.** The constraint from the strategy holds — this is *buyer/diaspora + cultural-awareness* reach, not the rural-seller channel (those are won on trust, §2.2). But the brand the memes build is what makes every other conversation warmer.

**Mimoo — the consumer brand name.** Mimoo (≈ *moo*) is the product/brand the masses know — short, friendly, repeatable, engineered for virality in a way "ZimLivestock" (the descriptive, B2B-facing name) never could be. The whole job of the name is to be a **meme before it's a pitch**: easy enough that a high-schooler repeats it and a cattle-owning dad hears it at dinner. That word-of-mouth path — kid to dad — is the exact mechanism behind your vision, and the name is the vehicle. A mascot and a jingle can sit *under* the name to carry it, but the asset is the word itself: **Mimoo.**

**Virality mechanics (the metrics that matter — learn these cold).** The algorithm does not reward likes. In rough priority:
1. **Completion / watch-time** — the hook in the first **1–2 seconds** decides ~90% of reach. Open on the punchline or the tension, never a logo.
2. **Shares** — the reach multiplier. Engineer a *share trigger*: "*tag the uncle who overpaid lobola.*"
3. **Comments** — gently bait them ("*Brahman or Mashona — settle it*").
4. **Saves**, then **likes** (vanity, last).
- **Humour is the engine** (your point, and it's correct): laughter creates a memory, a memory creates a share. So make people laugh, then give them someone to tag.
- **Volume to find the hit, then double down.** Post a lot (you said more is better — true *here*). Most posts die; one in twenty hits. When one hits, **repost it everywhere and make three more like it.** Repetition again.
- Trend-jack: ride trending sounds/formats with cattle-auction twists. Free distribution.

**Content concepts (commit to the bold, funny direction — these are starting ammo):**
- **"POV: you named the cow and now it's Lot #14."** Emotional-comedy. Universally Zimbabwean.
- **The auctioneer-speed chant** — subtitled. "*Nobody understands him but the cattle.*" Trend-jackable against fast-talking formats.
- **Lobola economics** (tasteful): "*explaining to the in-laws that 8 cows can now be one EcoCash notification.*"
- **EcoCash-notification ASMR** — the sound of getting paid before the dust settles. "*Sold a beast. Money landed before I reached the gate.*"
- **Breed-rivalry stan accounts** — Brahman vs Mashona vs Tuli like football fandom. Comment war = free reach.
- **"This bull sold for more than your car."** Flex/aspiration. Screenshot the price.
- **Diaspora wholesome:** "*Bought my dad a bull from London for his birthday.*" Tag-bait + diaspora-buyer demand-gen in one.
- **Mimoo's hot takes / wisdom** — a recurring character bit. The line kids repeat.
- **A Mimoo jingle** — short, catchy, dumb enough to stick. The kids-to-dad vector is a *sound*, not a slogan.

**Do-things-that-don't-scale on social too:** in the first weeks, *you* reply to every comment, DM people who share, join the rivalry in the comments. Manual engagement seeds the algorithm and the culture before any of it scales.

## PART 3 — Be lookup-able (your personal brand)

Cheap, fast, and load-bearing. Do it in Week 1.

**LinkedIn (sharpen, don't rebuild):**
- **Headline:** operator framing, not student. *e.g.* "Building ZimLivestock — digitising Zimbabwe's livestock auctions onto Paynow rails | Paynow."
- **About:** 4 lines — the problem you saw at a real auction, what you built, the traction language you can honestly use (MVP live, five channels, Paynow-settled), and a contact line.
- **Build-in-public posts:** 2–3/week through the sprint — the field visit, what you learned in the groups, a Mimoo teaser, the deck-in-progress. Repetition makes you look inevitable. By pitch day, a prospect who Googles you finds momentum.

**The one-page site (a day's work):** who you are, what ZimLivestock is, three proof points, a short demo clip/screenshots, and a contact button. A clean Vercel one-pager or even a Carrd/Notion site. The job is simple: *when they look you up, something credible is there.* Put the link in your LinkedIn, your email signature, and on the booth banner.

**The Elon arc, applied:** post your *own* progression in public. Early posts will be rough; by week three they'll be sharp. Letting people watch you get better is itself the credibility story — and it forces the reps.

## PART 4 — The pitch deck (5–6 slides) + reps protocol

Built from the **auction-house POV**: their problem → your fix → the money they're leaving → the ask. Short enough to deliver in **under 3 minutes.** Draft copy below; I'll build the actual `.pptx` once you pick the story (questions at the end).

| # | Slide | Job | Draft copy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | **The story (hook)** | Make them *feel* it in 15 seconds | *"My father runs finance at the central bank — and loves cattle more than spreadsheets. But he's never free on a sale-day Tuesday, so he buys blind on WhatsApp and overpays. There are thousands like him. **That's the demand leaking off your floor.**"* |
| **2** | **The problem (their floor)** | Name their pain in their words | "Your room is only as strong as who's in it. Every Saturday, buyers who can't make the deposit or the drive go to WhatsApp instead — unsettled, unverified, at lower prices. **Your demand is leaking to a group chat that can't even guarantee payment.**" |
| **3** | **Money on the table** | The number that stings | "[House] clears ~US$[X]/sale-day. A fuller room lifts prices. A 3% lift = **+US$[Y] GMV/yr** → **~US$[Z] more commission** — for less than one sale-day's takings a month." |
| **4** | **The solution** | You make *them* the winner | "ZimLivestock adds remote and diaspora bidders to your *live* floor — your brand, your auctioneer, your fees. Settled instantly on Paynow. You don't get disrupted by digital; **you become the digital floor.**" |
| **5** | **How it works + trust** | Make it real and safe | "Three steps. Five channels — web, WhatsApp, USSD, BillPay, Messenger. Digital police clearance + escrow so buyers trust it. Sellers paid to a Paynow ID — no bank details, no custody on you." |
| **6** | **The ask** | Low-risk close | "**One sale day. 90-day pilot, US$1,000 — credited to your contract if you stay.** Let me show you the buyers you're missing. *[contact + site]*" |

**Slide 1 story — locked (your father).** The real one, and the best one:

> *"My father is a Director of Finance at the Reserve Bank of Zimbabwe. He has spent his life around numbers — and his heart is in cattle. But he's never free on a sale-day Tuesday to drive three hours to the pen. So he doesn't buy — or he buys blind, through a middleman on WhatsApp, and overpays. There are thousands like him: doctors, engineers, civil servants, the diaspora — people with the money and the love for cattle, locked out by one thing: they can't be in the room. That missing buyer is the demand leaking off your floor. I built Mimoo to put him back in your room."*

It does four jobs at once: **credibility** (the RBZ name), **personification** (it makes the abstract "remote buyer" a real human), **flattery** (these are *your* missing high-value bidders, not strangers), and **warmth** (a son building for his dad). It also hands Slide 3 its number — those locked-out professionals are the recapturable demand. *(Anonymise to "a senior executive at the central bank" if you'd rather not name the role.)*

**Reps protocol (the Elon arc, compressed into 50 reps):**
1. **Record every rep** on your phone. No exceptions — unrecorded reps don't count.
2. Watch it back at 1.5×. Note **one** thing: a filler word, a slow patch, a place your energy dropped.
3. Fix that **one** thing next rep. One variable at a time.
4. **3 reps/day** through the sprint ≈ 50 before the first real meeting.
5. Pitch to **non-experts** (friends, family) and time it (≤3 min). Then ask them to *repeat your pitch back to you.* **If they can't, the deck is unclear — not them.** That's your clarity test.
6. Track: time, filler-count, "did they get it." Watch the curve bend. 2008-Elon → now-Elon is just this, done in public for years. You're doing it in three weeks on purpose.

## PART 5 — What to track, and the honest constraints

**Sprint scoreboard (update daily):** meetings booked · pitches delivered · reps logged · leads captured (seller/buyer/house, region, pain) · follow-ups sent · posts shipped · best-performing post (shares).

**Honest flags — so the plan stays real:**
- **3 weeks buys pipeline, not signatures.** Aim for booked meetings, a deck you own cold, ~50 reps, and a captured list. A pilot signature lands on the pilot clock, after.
- **Virality is a lottery you buy tickets in.** You cannot *guarantee* a hit; you raise the odds with volume + humour + share-triggers. Budget effort, not expectations — and when one post hits, *that's* where the energy goes.
- **Don't burn the groups.** The trust you're selling is the product. Infiltrate to learn and serve; the moment you spam, you're the scam they already complain about.
- **Personal brand is a multiplier, not the work.** A day on LinkedIn + a one-pager is enough. Don't let it eat the reps and the outreach — those are the job.
