## The one idea that organises everything

The platform has **one paying customer** (the auction house) and **six other actors** whose behaviour determines whether that customer succeeds. You do not engage them the same way, and — critically — **you do not engage them in parallel.** There is a strict order of operations:

```
PRE-LAUNCH        SELL              ENABLE                 LAYER
Paynow +          Auction house  →  Sellers + Buyers   →   Transporters
institutions      (B2B, paid)       (demand-gen)           (consumer line)
(credibility)     anchors-first     per signed catchment   downstream of volume
```

You cannot enable sellers before a house signs (the house owns them). You cannot run buyer demand-gen with no inventory to bid on. You cannot recruit transporters before there is delivery demand. **Engagement is sequenced, not broadcast** — and that sequencing is what keeps the cost sane.

### The marketing reframe (the load-bearing finding)

The instinct with "educate the masses" is national media — radio, billboards, agricultural-show stands at scale. In Zimbabwe, **for the rural cattle seller, that is the wrong and expensive channel.** Field-channel research is unambiguous: communal cattle owners are reached through **trust networks** — dip tanks, extension officers, farmer unions, vernacular radio, SMS — which are *low-cash and high-credibility*. Paid digital and national commercial radio reach **buyers and diaspora**, not communal sellers.

So the consumer brand is built **bottom-up, one catchment at a time**, riding the trust of institutions farmers already believe — not top-down through a media spend that would erase Year-1 surplus. **This is the field-honest execution of the demand-gen choice, and it fits the budget already in the model.** A maximal national-brand version is costed in §7 as a surplus-gated upgrade, so the trade-off is explicit.

## 1. Auction houses — the sell

**What they are.** The 40–60 houses running regular sales (Mashonaland, Matabeleland, Midlands). The paying tenant: one-off onboarding + monthly subscription + 0.75% take. Anchors-first.

**What they want.** More bidders on the floor (more demand → higher hammer prices), less settlement admin, faster payouts, a modern brand, and reach to remote/diaspora buyers they structurally cannot serve today.

**What they fear** (and you must pre-empt it):
- *"You'll disintermediate me."* → No. The house stays the brand of record; we are white-label rails behind *their* floor. RLS tenant isolation means their book is theirs.
- *"Async bidding kills the FOMO that lifts my prices."* (The real objection — surfaced in field research: the auctioneer's speed creates urgency.) → We **add remote bidders to the live floor**, not replace it. Hybrid: the auctioneer still runs the room; digital bids stream in alongside. More demand, not less urgency.
- *"I lose my 12% fees."* → You keep your fees. We undercut nobody's commission; our 0.75% is on settled GMV on top, and it buys you a bigger, settled, lower-default buyer pool.

**How we engage** (high-touch, founder-owned, reference-led):
1. **Saturday on-floor visit** — watch a real sale day, quantify their settlement pain (cash handling, no-shows, slow payouts).
2. **Discovery call** ([discovery-call-script.md](./discovery-call-script.md)) → diagnose, not pitch.
3. **90-day pilot** at US$1,000 (creditable) — prove ≥30% of GMV settling digitally on a real floor.
4. **Convert** to 12-month tier. The first anchor becomes the reference that de-risks the next — *one signed anchor is worth more than any ad.*

**Channels:** in-person floor visits · warm intros from Paynow BD · the ZLPA conference (sponsor + speak: "digital infrastructure for the modern auction floor").

**Cost:** BD travel + pilot subsidy + conference. Inside the model's BD/travel line.

**Metric:** houses signed vs the anchors-first ramp (3A/1B/1C in Y1); pilot→convert rate (target ≥1-in-2 piloted).

## 2. Sellers — the activation (two segments, two approaches)

Sellers are **not one stakeholder.** Treating them as one is the mistake.

### 2a. Communal / smallholder farmers (the underserved core)
Rural, feature-phone, low digital trust, sell a handful of beasts a year. The segment the manual floor's US$1,000 deposit and 12% fees push onto unsettled WhatsApp groups — *the segment we exist to recapture.*

- **What they want:** payout **speed and certainty** ("money in my EcoCash before I leave the pen"), more buyers for their few animals, no bank account required.
- **What they fear:** digital scams, not getting paid, technology they can't see.
- **The unlock:** Paynow-ID payout (no bank details — addresses demo-panel ask #4), and **peer + institutional trust transfer**. A farmer believes their dip-tank committee, their extension officer, and Radio Zimbabwe — not an app.
- **How we engage:** *not direct acquisition by us.* When a house signs, we run a **co-funded catchment activation** in its province: a "digital sale-day" launch mobilised through dip-tank committees and ZFU clubs, vernacular onboarding, first-sale shadowing, and SMS/USSD follow-up.
- **Channels:** dip tanks (4,183 nationally — standing committees, fixed dipping days) · AGRITEX/vet field days · ZFU farmer clubs · **Radio Zimbabwe** (Shona/Ndebele, top rural reach) · community radio (Skyz Metro, Diamond FM, YaFM, Nyaminyami) · bulk SMS + USSD (works on any phone, no data).

### 2b. Dealers / resellers (the commercial repeat seller)
Field research: floor attendees are *mostly* dealers, not end consumers. Commercial, repeat, multi-house, smartphone-capable.

- **What they want:** volume, margin, speed, the ability to work several houses from one dashboard, bulk listing.
- **How we engage:** at the floor + digitally; a dealer dashboard and bulk-list flow. Lower friction, not hand-holding.
- **Channels:** web/PWA · WhatsApp bot · on-floor.

**Cost:** a per-house activation envelope (agent days, SMS, vernacular radio in *that* province, print) — see §7. Repeated each time a house signs, never as a national blast.

**Metric:** % of a signed house's sellers listing digitally (this *is* the model's adoption number, 10.5→13.7%) within 90 days.

## 3. Buyers — the demand-gen (where the paid budget lives)

Two segments, and the **new** one is the growth.

- **On-floor dealers** — already there; convert them to digital bidding (covered in §2b's mirror).
- **Remote / diaspora / employed buyers** — the segment the deposit barrier and travel **exclude today**. Field research: auction prices beat WhatsApp precisely because of trust infrastructure. We extend that infrastructure to people who can't attend. **This is the consumer brand the demand-gen choice is really about — a *buyer*-acquisition brand.**

- **What they want:** bid from anywhere, a **low refundable deposit** (small escrow vs US$1,000), and trust that the animal is real and will arrive.
- **What they fear:** paying for an animal they can't inspect, non-delivery, scams.
- **The unlock:** digital police clearance (it's real) + **Bisafe escrow** (funds held until delivery confirmed — demo-panel ask #5) + transport/delivery. Lower the deposit gate to widen the funnel.
- **How we engage:** *this* is where paid demand-gen runs — and only here is paid media efficient.
- **Channels:** Meta (Facebook/IG) + WhatsApp **click-to-chat** ads (Zimbabwe CPM ~US$1–3; US$150–500/mo buys 100k–400k impressions) · **Star FM** (urban/younger buyers) · **diaspora Facebook groups** (UK/SA/AU Zimbabwean communities — diaspora-funded purchases for family back home) · the **ZAS national show** (~250k visitors) for visibility and buyer relationships.

**Cost:** the paid-media line — modest in absolute terms (see §7).

**Metric:** registered buyers, **bids placed from off-floor**, and deposit-gate conversion (the leading indicator that the new segment is real).

## 4. Transporters — the recruit (downstream, never Year 1)

**What they are.** The dispatch network that makes the transport consumer-revenue line real. **We own no trucks** — we coordinate; they haul and price the haul.

- **What they want:** route-batched full loads, **guaranteed payment via Paynow**, more jobs, zero customer-acquisition cost (we bring the demand).
- **How we engage:** recruit local cattle-truck owners region-by-region, **only where digital volume already exists.** Price them generously early to build reliability (per the v3.0 risk table) — a bad early delivery poisons the whole consumer line.
- **Channels:** direct outreach · house referrals · transporter associations.
- **Cost:** low — vetting + onboarding + small early guarantees.
- **Metric:** vetted transporters per active region · on-time delivery rate · transport attach-rate (model: 5%→24%).
- **Sequencing rule:** do not build a national transport network in Year 1. It is a Year-2+ line that follows GMV.

## 5. Police & Vet — the integrate (gatekeeper → trust multiplier)

**What they are.** Mandatory clearance. Field research: a policewoman clears cattle on-site; movement permits and dip records run through the **Department of Veterinary Services**. *This must be digitised, never skipped.*

- **What they want:** a digital, auditable clearance trail that **reduces stock theft** (a major Zimbabwe problem), and less paperwork.
- **How we engage:** pilot the digital police-clearance workflow with **one station + one house**; secure Veterinary Services buy-in for digital movement permits. Regulatory relationship-building — MoU-based, **months not weeks.**
- **Channels:** formal — ZRP stock-theft unit · Dept. of Veterinary Services · relevant Ministry.
- **Cost:** travel + in-kind; low cash.
- **Metric:** clearances processed digitally · a signed endorsement/MoU.
- **Why it matters beyond compliance:** a police/vet endorsement is a **trust multiplier for every other stakeholder** — it is the single strongest credibility signal you can show a sceptical communal farmer.

## 6. ZLPA / ZFU / AGRITEX — the partner (institutional reach + credibility)

The cheapest, highest-trust reach into the rural seller base runs through institutions that already exist and are *under-resourced* — i.e. open to a partner that brings logistics.

| Institution | Scale | What they want | Our ask | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Zimbabwe Farmers Union (ZFU)** | >1M farming households, clubs to village level; prior digital tie-up (EcoFarmer/Econet — precedent exists) | Modern services for members | Co-branded member outreach, SMS to clubs, roadshow access | Negotiated; low US$-hundreds–thousands/campaign |
| **AGRITEX** (extension) | ~4,790 officers — trusted last-mile, chronically short on transport/fuel | To serve farmers they can't currently reach | MoU: we supply fuel/transport/airtime; officers introduce the platform at trainings | ~US$0 fee + in-kind activation |
| **ZLPA / livestock producers' body** | Industry convening + credibility | Sector modernisation | Sponsor + speak at conference; warm leads | Sponsorship (low-thousands) |
| **Veterinary / dip-tank committees** | 4,183 dip tanks, each a standing committee | Less theft, organised records | Activation node for catchment launches | Per-session, single-digit–low-tens USD |

- **How we engage:** MoUs and in-kind logistics support, not cash transfers. AGRITEX and unions are *force multipliers* — one officer reaches a community we'd spend weeks finding.
- **Metric:** warm leads per conference (target ≥5) · officer-led demos run · member sign-ups via union channels.
- **Honest flag:** these are months-long relationship builds with government-adjacent bodies. **Start them pre-launch** (§ sequencing) because they will not move on demand.

## 7. Paynow — the align (the spine and the internship-return ask)

Covered fully in [gtm-brief.md](./gtm-brief.md). In one line: Paynow is the settlement/payout rail on all five channels, the strategic moat (the money layer three of four national livestock initiatives lack), *and* a referral channel. ### Optional upgrade — aggressive national brand (surplus-gated)

The maximal "consumer brand farmers seek out" version, layered on top:

| Add-on | ~US$/yr | Effect |
|---|---:|---|
| Sustained national commercial radio (Star FM/ZiFM flights) | +6,000 | Urban + buyer brand awareness |
| ZAS + 2 provincial show stands | +4,000 | ~250k+ visitor visibility |
| Heavier paid digital (US$500/mo) | +4,000 | Diaspora + urban buyer scale |
| Regional agent teams (3–4 more) | +6,000 | Multi-province simultaneous activation |
| Creative / vernacular video production | +2,000 | Brand assets, social content |
| **Add-on total** | **~+22,000** | |

**The honest trade-off:** running this in **Year 1** turns surplus from **+12,795 to ≈ −9,000** — it would consume the entire thin Y1 cushion (~3.5 weeks of opex) and dip into the working-capital buffer. **Not recommended before Year 3**, when surplus is +65k and can fund it from cash. The recommendation is to **earn the national brand from regional proof** — build it bottom-up as houses come online, and switch on the media layer once the surplus can pay for it without touching the buffer.

## How marketing pays for itself (the upside, kept out of the base case)

Demand-gen is the one lever that moves the model's two conservative numbers:

- **Adoption** is held field-honest at 10.5–13.7% (a *maturity floor*, not a growth lever). Effective catchment activation is what could push a mature house above that — but we do **not** bake unproven adoption upside into the numbers (consistent with the v3.0 discipline). It's upside, flagged, not modelled.
- **Transport attach** (5%→24%) rises faster the more remote buyers we acquire — they structurally cannot self-haul, so buyer demand-gen *directly* feeds the consumer line.
- **Hammer prices** rise with more bidders, which makes the *house* more money — the strongest retention argument and the cheapest referral engine.

So the budget above is a floor that protects surplus; any over-performance on adoption or attach is margin we let the field prove before we count it.

## Risks specific to engagement

| Risk | Why it bites | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| **Institutional channels move slowly** | ZFU/AGRITEX/Vet/Police are government-adjacent; MoUs take months. | Start them in **Phase 0**, before you need them. Treat in-kind, not cash. |
| **Activating sellers before a house signs** | Wasted spend, broken promise (nothing to transact on). | Hard rule: **no catchment spend until a house is signed.** Demand-gen is gated on the sell. |
| **Paid media aimed at communal sellers** | Burns cash on an audience that's off Meta and tuning out commercial radio. | Paid media is **buyer/diaspora only**; sellers via trust channels. The whole §1-reframe. |
| **Transport over-built early** | Idle trucks, bad first deliveries poison the consumer line. | Recruit **only where volume exists**; Year-2+; price transporters generously early. |
| **Unquoted line items blow the budget** | Radio/ZAS/USSD/partnership fees aren't published. | Get **written quotes** on the five thin lines before committing; budget holds ranges, not point bets. |

## Assumptions & data confidence

Channel costs are grounded in 2025–26 Zimbabwe field-channel research. **Well-sourced** (commit-ready): bulk SMS (~US$0.0026/SMS Econet bundle), USSD per-session (US$0.05 regulated cap), print (5,000 A5 flyers ~US$190; pull-up banner ~US$70; tee ~US$2.50–7), wage benchmarks (rural field agent ~US$150–300/mo), dip-tank count (4,183), AGRITEX staff (~4,790), ZFU scale (>1M households), ZAS attendance (~250k), Meta CPM (~US$1–3, regional inference). **Get a written quote before committing** (no public rate card exists): all radio spot rates, ZAS/provincial show stand prices, USSD shortcode setup + monthly rental, and AGRITEX/ZFU partnership fees. Budget figures carry ranges, not point estimates, on the thin lines.
